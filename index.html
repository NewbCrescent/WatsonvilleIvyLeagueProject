About WILP

The Watsonville Ivy League Project (WILP) opens pathways to Ivy League universities for high-achieving, low-income students, embodying our commitment to equal opportunity and transformative education. Rooted in a community symbolized by the resilient strawberry, WILP nurtures future leaders through exposure to world-class academic environments.

Goals

Opportunity

The Watsonville Ivy League Project provides high-achieving, low-income students from selected high schools an opportunity to visit Ivy League universities, broadening their educational horizons and exposing them to elite academic environments.

Education

Students receive educational tours and insights into the Ivy League system, including interactions with alumni, which offer a realistic view of life at these prestigious institutions, supplemented by cultural experiences.

Impact

This program is an investment in the academic futures of participating students, with community support and fundraising playing a key role in making this transformative experience accessible.

2023 - 2024 WILP

In this new year, the Watsonville Ivy League Project proudly announces the selection of 12 accomplished students to join our transformative program. We are currently in the vital fundraising stage to support these members, actively preparing for their future educational journeys to prestigious Ivy League universities.

Last Year's WILP

During their seven-day journey, the students of the Watsonville Ivy League Project visited nine prestigious universities, including Harvard, MIT, and Yale, where they participated in campus tours and admissions presentations. They engaged with alumni from their high schools now at these Ivy League colleges and enjoyed cultural experiences, like a Broadway show in New York and historical tours in Boston and Philadelphia. This enriching trip, undeterred by rain, proved to be an unforgettable educational adventure.
